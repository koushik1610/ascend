# Prep-Doc — Northwind Health · Staff Product Designer

**Hook (line 1):** "I build design systems engineering actually adopts — and I'd rather own that
end-to-end at a health platform than design one more pretty Figma library no one ships."

## Role thesis (why I win this loop)
Northwind is hiring a Staff IC to bring design-systems rigor and accessibility to a multi-team health
SaaS platform. That is the exact shape of my last four years: I built and scaled a 120-component
tokenized system, drove adoption across 5 teams without authority, and took it to WCAG 2.1 AA while
pairing through implementation. Health adds regulatory weight to accessibility — which plays to my
strength, not against it.

## Talking points (expanded)
1. **Design system at scale (E1 / M1–M3).** 120 tokenized components + Storybook, 5 teams, ~32%
   adoption lift. Lead with this — it's the JD's #1 ask.
2. **Accessibility-first (E3 / M11–M12).** WCAG 2.1 AA across the library; eng pairing cut spec-drift
   defects ~44%. Frame accessibility as a health-domain advantage.
3. **Governance that scales (E2).** Contribution model + rubric so teams extend, not fork. This is the
   Staff differentiator vs. a Senior who just makes components.
4. **Data-informed range (E4 / M8).** B2B dashboard redesign moved NPS 22→41 — shows I design with
   evidence, partnering with data (not pretending to be a data scientist).
5. **IC leadership (E9 / M9).** Mentors 3–4, runs crits. The honest answer to "have you led."

## Gaps + rehearsed handling lines (verbatim)
- **No manager title:** "I'm an IC multiplier — I mentor 3–4 designers and run our crit practice, and
  I'm specifically targeting Staff IC scope, not a management track. If this role needs a people
  manager, I want to be upfront that that's not what I'm pursuing."
- **New to health domain:** "I've shipped in regulated-adjacent fintech, so compliance constraints
  aren't new. I'd spend my first weeks learning Northwind's specific regulatory and clinical
  constraints before touching the system — happy to walk through how I ramp on a new domain."
- **Not a data scientist:** "I'm a data *partner* — I read funnels and co-design experiments with
  PM/data. I don't build models, and I won't pretend to."
